Current research interests.

My current research focuses on agentic AI in data science, on how AI can improve the quality and efficiency of investment decisions, and on how AI can support better content creation with lower labor cost.

Working Paper

Decision-Rights Allocation: A Governance-Centric Perspective on LLM-Enabled Agentic Systems

LLM-enabled agents are increasingly deployed as components of task-and-decision processes that retrieve information, reason over context, call tools, coordinate with other agents, and act on behalf of humans. This paper asks a central design question: who has the right to make each consequential decision inside the process?

It frames agentic systems as allocations of decision rights among human actors, deterministic components, and stochastic LLM components. The framework provides a compact vocabulary for comparing designs and for deciding when LLMs should act, when infrastructure should constrain them, and when humans should retain or regain control.

Working Paper

Reducing LLM Decision Rights in Agentic AI Systems: A Distillation-Based Design Pattern

This paper proposes a practical response to broad LLM control: reduce the runtime decision rights of LLM components by distilling their previous decisions into local deterministic or semi-deterministic models.

The proposal does not reject LLMs. Instead, it uses them as teachers during the learning period, when broad adaptability is needed and the system has not yet accumulated enough local experience.
