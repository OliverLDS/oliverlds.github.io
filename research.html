Working Paper

Decision-Rights Allocation: A Governance-Centric Perspective on LLM-Enabled Agentic Systems

LLM-enabled agentic systems are increasingly deployed inside task-and-decision processes that retrieve information, reason over context, call tools, coordinate with other agents, and execute actions on behalf of humans. Existing conceptual work has usefully classified these systems by external behavior, architectural components, cognitive design patterns, autonomy levels, and multi-agent governance forms.

Those frameworks clarify what agentic systems contain and how they behave, but they often leave implicit a design choice that becomes central once stochastic language models are connected to organizational processes: which party has the effective right to make each consequential decision? This paper proposes decision-rights allocation as a compact internal-mechanism perspective for understanding and designing LLM-enabled agentic systems.

Working Paper

Decision-Trace Distillation for Reducing LLM Decision Rights in Agentic AI Systems

LLM-enabled agentic systems increasingly give large language models operational authority over workflow transitions: interpreting goals, selecting evidence, routing subtasks, choosing tools, evaluating intermediate outputs, writing memory, and deciding whether a process should continue, stop, retry, or escalate. This flexibility is useful when tasks are open-ended or changing, but it also makes consequential workflow behavior harder to reproduce, inspect, audit, and bound.

This paper proposes *decision-trace distillation* as a design pattern for reducing unnecessary runtime decision rights of LLM components without removing the adaptability that makes them valuable. During a learning period, the LLM holds broader authority and the system records its decisions together with task context, candidate options, rationales, human feedback, downstream outcomes, overrides, uncertainty signals, and scope or risk tags.

Working Paper

agentr: Practical Scaffolding for Coding-AI Workflows

agentr is an R package for scaffolding coding-AI workflows through inspectable task specifications, review artifacts, and autonomy boundaries. Its contribution is not to provide another model wrapper, chat interface, or autonomous execution engine.

Instead, it supplies a project-local structure through which external coding AIs---including repository-aware assistants, command-line agents, and GUI/API-backed model tools---can help design and implement work while leaving intermediate decisions visible. The package focuses on three related capabilities.
