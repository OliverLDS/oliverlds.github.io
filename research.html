Working Paper

From Human Workflow to Agentic Workflow: Transitional Scaffolding with the R Package agentr

This paper introduces agentr as an R-based framework for the cognitive and human-interaction core of intelligent-agent scaffolding. The central claim is that the hard part of building useful agents is often not raw execution, but externalizing tacit expert workflow into explicit design artifacts that can be revised and approved.

The manuscript makes three main contributions: it frames the transition from human workflow to agentic workflow as transitional scaffolding, proposes a two-axis design model separating subsystem composition from workflow structure, and presents the current package implementation with before-and-after refinement demonstrations. Two illustrative cases center on economic analysis with visualization and market investment suggestion writing.
