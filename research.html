Current research interests.

My current research focuses on agentic AI in data science, on how AI can improve the quality and efficiency of investment decisions, and on how AI can support better content creation with lower labor cost.

Working Paper

Decision-Rights Allocation: A Governance-Centric Perspective on LLM-Enabled Agentic Systems

LLM-enabled agentic systems are increasingly deployed inside task-and-decision processes that retrieve information, reason over context, call tools, coordinate with other agents, and execute actions on behalf of humans. Existing conceptual work has usefully classified these systems by external behavior, architectural components, cognitive design patterns, autonomy levels, and multi-agent governance forms.

Those frameworks clarify what agentic systems contain and how they behave, but they often leave implicit a design choice that becomes central once stochastic language models are connected to organizational processes: which party has the effective right to make each consequential decision? This paper proposes decision-rights allocation as a compact internal-mechanism perspective for understanding and designing LLM-enabled agentic systems.

Working Paper

Reducing LLM Decision Rights in Agentic AI Systems: A Distillation-Based Design Pattern

Agentic AI systems increasingly assign decision authority to large language models (LLMs). This design choice improves scalability and adaptability because the same model can interpret new contexts, select tools, route workflows, and generate action plans without task-specific engineering.

However, it also introduces practical concerns about reliability, transparency, reproducibility, governance, and accountability. This paper proposes a design pattern for reducing the decision rights of LLM components without removing the benefits that make them useful.

Working Paper

agentr: Practical Scaffolding for Coding-AI Workflows

agentr is an R package for specifying, reviewing, and scaffolding agentic AI workflows. Its practical contribution is not to provide another model wrapper, chat interface, or autonomous execution engine.

Instead, it supplies a structured layer for using external coding AIs---including repository-aware coding assistants, command-line coding agents, and GUI/API-backed model tools---inside inspectable development workflows. agentr standardizes task-local artifacts for workflow design, memory, curated knowledge, interface expectations, autonomy boundaries, and visual review.
